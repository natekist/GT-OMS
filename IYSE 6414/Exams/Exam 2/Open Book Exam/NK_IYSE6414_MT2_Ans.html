Background¶

In this exam, you will be considering various socio-economic attributes of communities within different counties in the US and use the given data to predict total number of violent crimes per 1000 population (ViolentCrimesPerPop).

The data in "crime.csv" contains 1993 data points with the following 12 attributes:

  1. PersPerOccupHous: mean persons per household
  2. PctHousOwnOcc: percent of households owner occupied
  3. MalePctDivorce: percentage of males who are divorced
  4. FamInc: Family income category: Low/Medium/High (Qualitative)
  5. PctRecImmig8: percent of population who have immigrated within the last 8 years
  6. NormPopulation: Normalized population for community
  7. Rent: Median Rent category:Low/Medium/High (Qualitative)
  8. PctPopUnderPov: percentage of people under the poverty level
  9. PctLess9thGrade: percentage of people 25 and over with less than a 9th grade education
  10. PctUnemployed: percentage of people 16 and over, in the labor force, and unemployed
  11. NumStreet: number of homeless people counted in the street
  12. ViolentCrimesPerPop: total number of violent crimes per 1000 population
